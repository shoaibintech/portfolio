Architecture notes

Good systems make
the complex feel calm.

A practical view of how I approach product architecture: create clear boundaries, make failure survivable, and leave a useful trail of signals.

01

Design around domains

I start with product workflows and ownership boundaries, then shape services and APIs around those domains. It keeps systems easier to change as the product evolves.

02

Make resilience intentional

Queues, retries, idempotency, caching, and carefully chosen consistency boundaries turn fragile workflows into systems that recover gracefully.

03

Observe before guessing

Tracing, metrics, structured logs, and meaningful dashboards make production behavior visible—so decisions are based on evidence rather than intuition.

04

Scale the bottleneck

I look for the constraint first: a slow query, a chatty dependency, an expensive computation, or a busy queue—then solve that specific problem cleanly.

My default toolkit

NestJS + AWS + event-driven patterns + observable operations.

Not a prescription—just a proven starting point for modern product teams that need to move quickly without building a house of cards.
